Full Stack Developer

I have spent most of my working life working in data teams in roles at UCAS, an online university in Australia, the Ministry of Education in New Zealand, and managing a team for a marketing company in Scotland. It was my experience working as part of an agile project team in New Zealand, tasked with creating a new school infrastructure database, that got me interested in software development. In my roles of Regression Tester and Data Quality Analyst, I was able to work with some great people and understand the complexities of creating and launching a software product. It was a lot of fun seeing the product evolve in Sprint Reviews, having Morning Tea get-togethers every Friday with the team, and having the opportunity to train staff in regional offices across New Zealand during the migration phase.

After a lot of reflection during the pandemic, I decided to make a career change and return to university. I had studied at university before, although had never achieved a Bachelor's degree. I considered taking a Computer Science or Digital Media program, although opted to take an innovative entrepreneurship program that was adopted from Finland just over a decade ago. This program was called Business (Team Entrepreneurship) and involved working in a team of 10-15 people, with the aim of achieving annual business targets through executing an effective strategy. I had some ideas of products I wanted to build and this program gave me the freedom to do this, whilst also testing their value with customers.

In my 1st year on the program, I created a discount website aimed at helping students save money at local food and beverage businesses. I was able to attract business customers who saw students as a good market to help them bring in more revenue during quiet periods. At the end of the year, I decided to build a customer loyalty mobile app. I started learning to write code, opting to use Flutter SDK and Dart to make it simpler to develop for cross-platform and Firebase for the database. Over the next 2yrs I failed miserably, completely underestimating the complexities of teaching myself software development.

However, through hard work, I was still able to achieve a First Class Honours. I developed a lot of key competencies and learnings throughout this program, which make me a valuable team member, including:

  • Being able to lead without a title
  • Understanding the importance of following processes
  • Respecting the motivations of peers and not pulling them in a direction they don't want to go
  • Working effectively in a team, including communicating well with difficult individuals, and creating a psychologically safe environment for all
  • Providing constructive feedback that allows team members to reflect on their performance
  • Making effective decisions that will make the business more successful
  • Managing my time and priorities well to ensure work is completed ahead of deadline

Still determined to become competent at programming, after my degree, I enrolled in an in-person 16wk Full Stack bootcamp in Bath from July-October 2024. This program helped me to address my weaknesses and enabled me to learn in a structured way, initially focusing on HTML/CSS, then building projects with PHP/JavaScript, and next working with libraries and frameworks. This program vastly improved my programming competence thanks to the excellent training I received and well thought through curriculum. I am now looking forward to joining a company to demonstrate the skills I have gained from both of these programs, to improve further, and to help the business grow.
